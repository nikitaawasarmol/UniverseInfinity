1. Raven Paradox

The raven paradox, also known as Hempel's paradox, Hempel's ravens, or rarely the paradox of indoor ornithology, is a paradox arising from the question of what is supposed to be evidence for a statement. Observing objects that are neither black nor ravens may formally increase the likelihood that all ravens are black even though, these observations are unrelated.

The paradox is as follows -

"(1) All ravens are black.
(2) If something is not black, then it is not a raven."

Given a general statement such as all ravens are black, a form of the same statement that refers to a specific observable instance of the general class would typically be considered to proving evidence for that general statement. 3. Crocodile Paradox

Crocodile paradox, also known as crocodile sophism, is a paradox in logic in the same family of paradoxes as the liar paradox.


The Crocodile Paradox says that -


"One day a crocodile abducts a kid. His mother request the crocodile to release the kid. Crocodile says "You have to tell what i am thinking about the release of child that is am I thinking to release him or not.? If you guessed correctly I'll release him." Here is the paradox.
  • If the Mother replies "Yes" to the question of crocodile and the crocodile was not thinking to release the kid then they both disagree and kid would not be released

  • If the mother replies "No" to the question, then crocodile won't release the kid and keep him even though he didn't want to."

Here its all about the perception and how we understand the meaning of world. In first case where the mother says Yes to the question, the crocodile may lie and say that he wasn't thinking to release him in order to create a disagreement and as per the terms he said he'll keep the kid.

Whereas in the other case where the mother says No to the question the crocodile may again lie but this time he'll take the answer of the mother as an Order rather than as a statement. So regardless of what he's thinking he'll keep the kid irrespective of his terms.

4. Twin Earth Paradox

The twin earth paradox deals with the concept of Multiverse. Here's what it says -

"Consider another Earth as Earth-2. All elements that we have on Earth are same on Earth-2, except it doesn't have what we call Water(H2O). Instead of H 2O, they have the liquid XYZ (we call their liquid as XYZ). Those people from Earth-2 call their liquid as water. So if both the Earth's are refering to water are they refering to same thing ?"

Twin Earth is a thought experiment proposed by philosopher Hilary Putnam in his papers "Meaning and Reference" (1973) and "The Meaning of 'Meaning'" (1975). It is meant to serve as an illustration of his argument for semantic externalism, or the view that the meanings of words are not purely psychological. The Twin Earth thought experiment was one of three examples that Putnam offered in support of semantic externalism, the other two being what he called the Aluminum-Molybdenum case and the Beech-Elm case. Since the publication of these cases, numerous variations on the thought experiment have been proposed by philosophers.

6. The Information Paradox

The Information Paradox is related to the concept of black holes. It says that -

"When any object is destroyed by a Black Hole its property/information is lost, but according to quantum physics information cannot be lost in a universe. So what happens to that piece of information ?"

Information is property of arrangement of particles. Suppose a bunch of carbon atoms are arranged in such a way that it forms the element Graphite. Now rearrange the carbon atoms and we'll get diamond. The fundamental is same, its arrangement changes everything.


This is how we can create different carbon components by just rearranging few carbon atoms.



Information tells us the history of object & how they're different from each other. Black Hole does the opposite. They take different things and make them the same. They destroy information which create the paradox. The speculation for this paradox are -

  • Information is destroyed - After entering inside the black hole, it maybe possible that our theories and belief regarding information of an object doesn't work inside. So it maybe posssible that the information actually gets destroyed. In such case we should rebuild our laws from scratch.
